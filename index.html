MIMOSA

MIMOSA is an ANR-funded research project dedicated to knee osteoarthritis (gonarthrosis), a major public health priority. The project develops transparent, trustworthy AI that combines X-ray and MRI to improve severity assessment, early diagnosis, and prediction of progression.

Why MIMOSA?

In current practice, knee OA is largely evaluated from X-rays by visual grading of joint space narrowing and osteophytes. This assessment can be inaccurate, insensitive to early changes, and requires experienced observers to be reproducible. MIMOSA addresses this gap with multimodal AI and explainable outputs.

About

Knee osteoarthritis (gonarthrosis) is a progressive degenerative disease and a public health priority. Diagnosis and follow-up are commonly based on radiographic inspection of joint space width and osteophytes. However, early structural changes can be subtle, and manual grading may suffer from limited sensitivity and inter-observer variability. MIMOSA develops machine learning methods—leveraging complementary information from X-ray and MRI—to support reliable severity estimation and early-stage detection, while providing transparent outputs suitable for clinical research workflows.

Clinical motivation

Improve reproducibility and sensitivity of imaging-based assessment, especially for early and subtle disease changes.

Scientific question

How can we predict the onset and progression of knee OA from radiographs, enhanced by multimodal imaging and learning?

Expected outcome

A transparent CADx system that supports automated severity evaluation and structured, interpretable reporting.
